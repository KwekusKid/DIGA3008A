Reflections on the readings and my experience of the internet today

The readings I selected was 'proposal for a universal electronic system and archive(from literary machines 1981)'. This reading looked at and expanded on the idea presented by Ted Nelson of a ever expanding computer network that contains all the literature of the world. Basically the worlds greatest archive, it explores how the network would be made on both a technical and conceptual level. He called the project Xanadu and wanted it to hold all literature. And to explore that the reading looked into what literature is and the idea of a linkage system. I really enjoyed how the reading defined literature as ongoing system of interconnecting documents. They talked about how all literature is connected even to the point of a child simply writing something on a piece of paper, because even what they wrote still comes from somewhere. That really shifted my perspective on writing and on a grander scale how I view humanity as a whole and our constant ability to learn and grow as a result of our connections to one another.

To connect all these different strains of literature, the reading explores the idea of a linkage system as well as a Electronic literary systems. Linkage systems were seen as invisible lines that connect different documents together. Think of the search bar feature on Tiktok. When on Tiktok and a video pops, if during that video and throughout the video's comment section a certain group of words or topic is brought up a significant amount of times then users have an easy way to search up said topic through two options. One is a grey search bar at the bottom of the video that when pressed by a user will automatically search the words on tiktok. And the other option is if a comment under the video clearly states the words/topic then it will become a blue comment which users can click on to instantly search tiktok for the words or topic. Weather or not users find exactly what they're looking for is another issue but I think this form of hypermedia is exactly what Ted wanted for his linkage system of Xanadu.

Another topic discussed in the reading I also really enjoyed was the idea of an electronic literary system. One thing the reading said about this system was that they wanted it to be simple and easy for users to navigate which I found really cool as a design goal because even now in 2025, 20 years since Nelsons theory was released and even more since the release of literary machines 1981 that has always been the goal of those involved in interactive media and user interface. But speaking on what the literary system was. Nelson wanted it that each document not only had multiple copies being easy to access and trace but every change and version to be tracked and saved as well. He believed this would be vital in developing literature as it would allow authors to expand on and look at the different possibilities and shapes all their writing could take. He wanted that changes and different versions of documents had their own index. This also applied to ideas beyond literature, pictures, videos as well as graphical data structures were to all have the system in place.
